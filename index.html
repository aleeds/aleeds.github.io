Metric Space Deformation

The following two canvas's represent a process that I am calling a "Metric Space Deformation". It is the projection of one metric space into another. Now, what does all this mean? First, I shall define a metric space. I shall make it look mathematical and nice once I figure out how to do that. A metric space is any Set S, with a function d: S X S -> R. The function d must have the following properties.

∀ x ∀ y d(x,y) ≥ 0
d(x,y) = 0 ⇔ x = y
And the triangle inequality holds.
A projection from one metric space to another is a deformation from Sd to Sd', where ∀ x, d(x,φ) = d'(x,φ), where φ is the origin. The following is the projection of d((x1,y1),(x2,y2)) = |x1 - x2| + |y1 - y2| to the standard Euclidean Metric.

Why is there a little rotated square on the untransformed plane? The metric that I am using for this is sometimes called the diamond metric. In that metric space, the diamond is a circle in that metric space. The circle on the other canvas is what you normally think of as a circle. The "square" from before, has been deformed into a circle
